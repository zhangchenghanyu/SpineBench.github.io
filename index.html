SpineBench: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Fine-Grained Analysis of Spinal Pathologies

Chenghanyu Zhang, Zekun Li, Peipei Li, Xing Cui, Shuhan Xia, Yiqiao Zhang, Qianyuan Zhuang
Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, University of California,Santa Barbara Peking Union Medical College Hospital
Paper arXiv Code Dataset

Abstract

With the increasing integration of Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) into the medical field, the effective evaluation of their performance is critical in specialized sub-domains. However, due to inherent disciplinary barriers, existing benchmarks primarily assess general medical tasks, inadequately capturing performance in nuanced areas like the spine. To address this, we introduce SpineBench, a comprehensive Visual Question Answering (VQA) benchmark specifically designed for the fine-grained analysis and evaluation of LVLM in the spinal domain. SpineBench comprises 64,878 QA pairs from 40,263 images, covering 12 spinal diseases through two critical clinical tasks: spinal disease diagnosis and spinal lesion localization. SpineBench is built by integrating and standardizing image-label pairs from open-source spinal disease datasets, and generates highly confusable distractors for each VQA pair using visual similarity analysis. We evaluate 12 leading LVLMs on SpineBench. The results reveal that these models generally exhibit poor performance in the spine domain, highlighting current limitations of LVLM and guiding future improvements in spinal medicine applications.

Dataset Overview

SpineBench integrates four public spinal disease datasets across X-ray and MRI modalities. The following table summarizes the data composition:

Dataset Modality Dimension Primary Diseases Count
BUU Spine X-ray 2D Spondylolisthesis 806
CSXA X-ray 2D Cervical Disorders 4,962
RSNA MRI 3D Degenerative Conditions 48,688
VinDr-SpineXR X-ray 3D Bone Lesions 23,202

VQA Generation Pipeline

Each Visual Question Answering (VQA) pair is generated in three steps:

Diverse question generation using Gemini-2.5-Pro.

Distractor options via visual similarity (SigLIP2-based).

Manual validation and integration of "Healthy" option.

Small Scale Benchamrk Selection

To facilitate fast evaluation, we constructed a subset containing 2,758 VQA pairs:

  • 1,758 for spinal disease diagnosis
  • 1,000 for lesion localization

Image selection is based on clarity, contrast, and label balance.
